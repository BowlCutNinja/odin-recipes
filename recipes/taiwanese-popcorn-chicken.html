How to make Taiwanese Popcorn Chicken

Description

This popcorn chicken recipe comes from Sue Li.

Servings: 4-6

Time: 30 minutes cooking, 1 hour marinating

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 teaspoons granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons Chinese five-spice powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground white pepper
  • 4 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 6 cups vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon Sichuan peppercorns or whole black peppercorns
  • 1 cup/115 grams tapioca flour
  • 1 cup fresh basil leaves

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, season chicken thighs with soy sauce, sugar, five-spice powder, white pepper and salt. Set aside to marinate for about 1 hour at room temperature, or refrigerate overnight.
  2. Get a medium sized pot and heat vegetable oil over medium heat to 350 degrees. Coarsely grind the peppercorns and mix with the remaining 2 teaspoons of kosher salt. Set aside.
  3. Toss the marinated chicken with tapioca flour and about 1 tablespoon of water until the tapioca flour looks like small beads and clings to the chicken. This will give the chicken a very crunchy crust.
  4. Add the chicken to the hot oil and fry until golden brown, 5-7 minutes. Once chicken is done cooking, transfer to a wire rack or paper towel-lined plate and season with the peppercorn mixture.
  5. Fry the basil leaves until translucent, 1 to 2 minutes, and scatter them over the fried chicken.
